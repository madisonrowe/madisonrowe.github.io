Why MIT?

MIT is ranked #4, tied with Yale University, in the nation for universities, and it is known for its math, science and engineering education. STEM being a large focus at this university, it automatically attracts me because of the opportunities I could have if I enrolled there. The indicator of student satisfaction, the freshmen retention rate, is 99%, which seems really high. The university is build in an urban setting which is nice since I have been living in an urban place my entire life and the commutes around this campus will feel familiar to me because of this.

Why Caltech?

Caltech is ranked #9 in the nation for universities. The most popular majors at Caltech are STEM-related, and this gives me the opportunity to have options of what to major in. Along with this, the class sizes are usually very small, meaning I would be able to communicate much easier with the professors and students around me. An indicator of student satisfaction is the freshmen retention rate, and at Caltech, it is 98%. This university is set in a suburban area, and this is also really enjoyable because there are lower chances of traffic and busy streets new the campus, and there is more space around the campus to enjoy. 96% of the students who enroll Caltech also live in college-owned buildings which makes commuting much easier.

Why IIT?

IIT is ranked #124 in the nation for universities, and focuses on engineering, computer and information sciences, and other technology-related fields, giving me a variety of pathways for possible majors. IIT is located in Chicago, making it easier for me to commute and commit to since I live in Chicago. Along with it being easier to commute, it will also save me money since moving across states will not be necessary. Almost half of the classes are fairly small regarding class-size, making it easier to communicate. The freshmen retention rate is at 91%, which though is a bit lower than the previous schools, but is still very high.
